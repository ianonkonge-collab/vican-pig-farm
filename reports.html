Farm Reports & Information

Comprehensive guide on pig feeding, vaccination, and habits

Pig Feeding Guidelines

Pregnant Sows

  • Daily Amount: 1.5kg per sow
  • Frequency: Twice daily (7 AM & 2:30 PM)
  • Feed Type: High-protein gestation feed
  • Water: Unlimited access, 15-20 liters/day
  • Nutrition: Rich in calcium, phosphorus, and vitamins

Gilts (Young Female Pigs)

  • Daily Amount: 1.25kg per gilt
  • Frequency: Twice daily (7 AM & 2:30 PM)
  • Feed Type: Grower feed with balanced nutrients
  • Water: Fresh water available at all times
  • Growth: Supports healthy development for breeding

General Feeding Best Practices

✓ Feed Quality: Always use fresh, high-quality feed free from mold and contaminants

✓ Feeding Schedule: Maintain consistent feeding times to establish routine

✓ Feed Storage: Store feed in dry, cool, and rodent-free environment

✓ Observation: Monitor feed consumption daily - reduced intake may indicate health issues

✓ Clean Feeders: Clean feeding equipment regularly to prevent disease

✓ Adjustments: Adjust feed amounts based on weight, age, and health status

Vaccination Schedule & Information

Essential Vaccinations

  • FMD (Foot and Mouth Disease): Annual booster vaccinations
  • PRRS (Porcine Reproductive): Critical for breeding herds
  • Swine Flu: Seasonal vaccination recommended
  • Parvovirus: Important for pregnant sows
  • Erysipelas: Protects against bacterial infections
  • Pneumonia: Especially for young pigs

Vaccination Timing

  • Piglets: First vaccination at 3-4 weeks of age
  • Boosters: Follow-up shots as per veterinary schedule
  • Pregnant Sows: Pre-breeding and pre-farrowing vaccines
  • Annual: Yearly vaccination for all adult pigs
  • Record Keeping: Document all vaccinations with dates
  • Consultation: Always consult with a veterinarian

Vaccination Best Practices

✓ Proper Storage: Keep vaccines refrigerated at 2-8°C

✓ Sterile Equipment: Use clean, sterilized syringes and needles

✓ Timing: Vaccinate healthy animals only - avoid sick or stressed pigs

✓ Post-Vaccination: Monitor pigs for 24-48 hours after vaccination

✓ Records: Maintain detailed vaccination records for each pig

✓ Professional Advice: Follow veterinarian recommendations for schedules

Pig Habits & Behavior

Daily Routines

  • Sleep 7-8 hours daily
  • Feeding occurs 2-3 times per day
  • Enjoy wallowing in mud for cooling
  • Active during morning and evening
  • Social animals - prefer group living

Behavioral Traits

  • Highly intelligent animals
  • Communicate through various sounds
  • Curious and explorative nature
  • Can be trained with positive reinforcement
  • Hierarchical social structure

Health Indicators

  • Active and alert demeanor
  • Lethargy indicates illness
  • Bright, clear eyes - sign of health
  • Good appetite shows wellness
  • Normal temperature: 38-40°C

Important Behavioral Notes

Environmental Needs

  • • Adequate space for movement and exercise
  • • Clean, dry bedding material
  • • Proper ventilation to prevent respiratory issues
  • • Shade and wallowing areas for temperature regulation
  • • Separate areas for sleeping, eating, and elimination

Stress Management

  • • Avoid sudden changes in routine or environment
  • • Minimize noise and disturbances
  • • Handle pigs gently and with patience
  • • Maintain consistent feeding schedules
  • • Watch for signs of stress: reduced appetite, aggression

Quick Reference Guide

Vital Signs

  • Normal Temperature: 38-40°C
  • Heart Rate: 70-100 beats/min
  • Respiration: 15-30 breaths/min
  • Body Condition: Ribs barely visible

Daily Routine

  • Morning Feed: 7:00 AM
  • Afternoon Feed: 2:30 PM
  • Cleaning: 11:00 AM & 4:00 PM
  • Observation: Multiple daily checks
